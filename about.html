About the Project

Disability Representation in Children’s Books is a project led by Dr. Carly Christensen, partially funded by Edith Lando Virtual Learning Centre and developed as part of the work of Canadian Institute for Inclusion and Citizenship (CIIC).

The project supports inclusive education by enhancing critical literacy around disability in children’s literature and curating resources that critically engage with disability representation.

Grounded in disability justice, the project moves beyond ableist and deficit-based narratives to celebrate disabled identities, experiences, and communities while supporting educators in creating more accessible, inclusive, and affirming classrooms.
Advisory Disability categories in this project are used as broad organizational tools for accessibility, searching, and educational use. They are not intended to reflect fixed or deficit-based understandings of disability. This project does not adhere to the medical model, but is grounded in disability justice, neurodiversity-affirming, and social model perspectives that recognize disability as shaped by social and environmental contexts.
In this project, “ND” is used to refer to both neurodiversity and neurodivergent experiences and identities.
